Game Dev Vibe Check

Your strategic playbook for hiring a Game Development Faculty.
Think of this less as an interview, and more as a co-op mission to find the best player.

The Game Plan: How to Run the Interview

A great interview has a natural flow. Use this timeline to guide your conversation:

  • First 5-10 Minutes: The Tutorial Level. Start with the Easy questions. Your goal here is to break the ice and check their passion. Are they actually excited about games, or just looking for a job?
  • Next 20-30 Minutes: The Main Quest. Dive into the Medium difficulty questions. This is where you test their core knowledge and practical skills. Can they actually do the job and, more importantly, can they explain *how* they do it?
  • Final 10-15 Minutes: The Boss Fight. If they've impressed you so far, hit them with one or two Hard questions. These test deep thinking, mentorship skills, and how they handle pressure. This is how you separate the good candidates from the great ones.
